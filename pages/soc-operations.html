Interview Q&A

Q: Walk me through your alert triage process.

"I follow a structured approach: Initial Assessment (30 sec) - read alert, check severity, identify affected system. Enrichment (1-2 min) - check asset criticality, IP reputation, threat intel. Validation (2-5 min) - verify context, check parent processes. Then decide: FP closes with documentation, low TP follows playbook, high TP escalates to L2 with findings."

Q: How do you reduce false positives?

"Multiple approaches: 1) Tune rules - add exclusions for known good patterns. 2) Improve context - enrich with asset criticality. 3) Correlation - alert on patterns not single events. 4) UEBA - baseline normal behavior. At Infosys, I reduced FP from 70% to 30% over 6 months."

Q: Describe your career progression L1 to Engineer.

"Started as L1 at Cognizant with Splunk - 50-100 alerts daily, learned pattern recognition, documented FP patterns, created quick reference guides. At Infosys (L2), managed Trellix and Cortex XDR for 300K endpoints - detection engineering, incident handling, XSOAR playbooks. Now at Siemens (L3) - leading DLP migration, architecting solutions, setting technical direction."
