Interview Q&A

Q: How do you prioritize vulnerabilities beyond just CVSS score?

"CVSS is a starting point, not the answer. I add context: Exploitability - is there a public exploit? On CISA KEV? Asset criticality - is it a crown jewel or test server? Internet-facing or internal? Compensating controls - is it behind a WAF? Segmented? Business impact - what happens if compromised? A critical vuln on an isolated test box is lower priority than a medium on a production payment server."

Q: What is CISA KEV and why is it important?

"Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog from CISA - list of CVEs with confirmed active exploitation in the wild. It's authoritative because it's backed by US government intelligence. If a vuln is on KEV, it jumps to top priority regardless of CVSS. Attackers ARE using it right now. Federal agencies must patch KEV vulns within set timeframes, but all orgs should treat it as critical."

Q: How would you handle a situation where patching would cause business disruption?

"Balance security and business needs. First, assess the real risk - is there active exploitation? Is it internet-facing? Then explore options: Virtual patching via WAF/IPS rules. Network segmentation to limit exposure. Enhanced monitoring for exploit attempts. Scheduled maintenance window at lowest impact time. Document the risk acceptance if delayed, with compensating controls and revised timeline. Never just ignore it."
