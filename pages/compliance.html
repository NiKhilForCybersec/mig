Interview Q&A

Q: Explain the NIST CSF functions.

"NIST CSF has 5 core functions - think of them as a security lifecycle: Identify (know your assets, risks, governance), Protect (implement safeguards - access control, encryption, training), Detect (monitor and identify incidents - SIEM, EDR), Respond (take action - containment, investigation), Recover (restore operations - DR, lessons learned). Each has categories and subcategories with specific controls."

Q: What's the difference between SOC 2 Type 1 and Type 2?

"Type 1 is a point-in-time assessment - auditor verifies controls are designed properly at a specific date. Type 2 covers a period (usually 6-12 months) and tests both design AND operating effectiveness - are controls actually working over time? Type 2 is more valuable because it shows sustained compliance, not just a snapshot."

Q: How do you approach a new compliance requirement?

"1) Gap analysis - map current controls to requirements. 2) Prioritize - address high-risk gaps first. 3) Remediate - implement missing controls. 4) Document - policies, procedures, evidence. 5) Test - verify controls work. 6) Monitor - continuous compliance. I also leverage control overlap - many frameworks share controls, so one control can satisfy multiple requirements."
